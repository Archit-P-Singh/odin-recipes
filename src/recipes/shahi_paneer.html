Shahi Paneer

Basic Introduction:

This Shahi Paneer is deliciously rich and creamy, making it one of the most popular recipes
in Mughlai cuisine. Fresh, unmelting cheese is married with a creamy gravy, perfect for serving
with naan or roti.

Shahi Paneer can literally be translated to “royal paneer.” This recipe is that of a rich, creamy
curry made with a base of nuts, onions, yogurt and spices.

There are some variations to make the recipe. Some Shahi Paneer recipes use tomatoes and butter.
I have opted not to use these ingredients, instead using yogurt to lighten up the dish considerably.

The Shahi gravy is full of fragrant flavors including onion, ginger, garlic, red chilli powder,
saffron and cardamom. Balanced out with thick, creamy yogurt, this lightened up version of Shahi
Paneer is perfect for special occasions.

Ingredients:

  • FOR PREPARATION:

    • 2 tablespoons chopped cashews
    • 1 tablespoon almonds
    • 1 tablespoon melon seeds (magaz)
    • ½ cup chopped onions
    • ½ teaspoon chopped ginger
    • ½ teaspoon chopped garlic
    • ½ cup fresh curd (yogurt)
    • 3 tablespoons ghee
    • 1 tej patta (Indian bay leaf)
    • 2 to 3 cloves
    • 2 to 3 green cardamoms
    • 1 black cardamom
    • 1 inch cinnamon
    • ½ teaspoon caraway seeds (shahi jeera)
    • ½ teaspoon red chilli powder
    • ¼ teaspoon garam masala powder
    • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
    • ½ to 1 teaspoon sugar (as required)
    • salt (as required)
    • ¼ teaspoon cardamom powder
    • crushed 15 to 20 saffron strands
    • paneer cubes (200 grams)
    • 1 tablespoon heavy cream
    • 1 teaspoon of kewra water (optional)

  • FOR GARNISH:

    • fresh coriander leaves
    • mint leaves
    • some saffron strands

Preparation:

  1. Take the nuts and seeds - 2 tablespoons chopped cashews, 1 tablespoon almonds, 1 tablespoon melon
    seeds (magaz) in a bowl and rinse them a couple of times with water.
    Drain all the water and set aside.



  2. Simmer the below listed ingredients in 1.5 cup water for for 8 to 10 minutes on medium-low heat.
    • ½ cup chopped onions
    • 2 tablespoons chopped cashews
    • 1 tablespoon almonds
    • 1 tablespoon melon seeds (magaz)
    • ½ teaspoon chopped ginger
    • ½ teaspoon chopped garlic



  3. Continue to cook until the onions become soft, then strain the stock and set aside for later use.
    When cooled, place the softened onions, nuts, seeds, ginger, garlic in a blender or grinder-mixer
    jar. You can peel the almonds if you prefer.



  4. Make a fine paste of the cooked onion mixture, adding 2 to 4 tablespoons of the strained water or
    as needed.



  5. Beat ½ cup fresh curd (yogurt) till smooth and set aside.



  6. Heat 3 tablespoons ghee in a pan over medium-low to medium heat. Let the ghee melt. You can even use
    oil or butter in place of ghee.



  7. Fry all the whole spices until they release their aroma in the ghee. The whole spices are listed below:
    • 1 tej patta (Indian bay leaf)
    • 2 to 3 cloves
    • 2 to 3 green cardamoms
    • 1 black cardamom
    • 1 black cardamom
    • ½ teaspoon caraway seeds (shahi jeera)

    Tip: Be very attentive here, as spices are delicate and can burn quickly if you aren't paying attention.

    Note that the whole spices are not eaten. If you prefer, you can remove the whole spices (after they
    are fried in the oil) excluding the caraway seeds, with a slotted spoon before adding the onion and nut
    paste.





  8. Add the onion and nuts paste.On a low to medium-low heat mix until the paste is uniform. Then sauté for 7-8
    minutes or until the fat separates at the sides of the paste.

    You will see that on further cooking the paste will thicken and become glossy. Keep stirring non-stop for even
    cooking. This will also prevent the paste getting burnt from the bottom.




  9. Now add ½ teaspoon red chilli powder or cayenne pepper, ¼ teaspoon garam masala powder and 1 teaspoon coriander powder.

    You could even replace red chilli powder with ¼ teaspoon white or black pepper powder.

    Stir to mix well.



  10. Reduce heat to a low or switch off the heat. Add the whisked yogurt, 1 to 1.5 cups of the strained stock and additional water,
    ½ to 1 teaspoon sugar and salt as required.

    Stir well and simmer on a low heat for 10 to 12 minutes, or until the gravy thickens. You can always alter the consistency of
    gravy by adding less or more water. Add ¼ teaspoon cardamom powder and crushed 15 to 20 saffron strands.




  11. Stir and add the paneer cubes (200 grams) and 1 tablespoon heavy cream. Add 1 teaspoon of kewra water and stir (optional step). If
    adding butter, you can add it here. Mix well and switch off the heat.

    Tip 1: If you are a vegan, you can easily substitute tofu for the paneer.
    Tip 2: If you prefer a richer version, feel free to add more cream or butter to the recipe where
    notated.




  12. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves or mint leaves or some saffron strands and enjoy with rice or flatbread.
